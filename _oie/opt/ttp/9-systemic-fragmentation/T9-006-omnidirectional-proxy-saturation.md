## Procedure
1. Identify a critical, documented unforced error or compliance blunder committed by a highly privileged external proxy (e.g., an external corporate lawyer or auditing firm).
2. Do not escalate linearly. Instead, package the evidence of this blunder and distribute it simultaneously across all operational vectors: the proxy's immediate supervisor, the executive board of the client company that hired them, and external regulatory/legal bodies.
3. Frame the communication to the client company as an urgent warning that their hired proxy is generating massive liability on their behalf.

## Goal
To execute a "Human DDoS" attack on an adversarial proxy. By forcing the proxy to simultaneously defend their actions to you, their own boss, their paying client, and the law, you completely paralyze their operational capacity and permanently sever the trust between the proxy and the target organization.

## Operational Logic
* External proxies survive by controlling the flow of information back to their clients, usually hiding their own mistakes.
* Attacking the proxy from multiple angles simultaneously shatters this compartmentalization.
* The target organization will immediately turn on their own proxy the moment they realize they are paying premium rates for a liability-generating blunder.
