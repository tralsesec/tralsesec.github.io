## Procedure
1. Identify the intersecting operational boundaries of a single administrative failure. Map out how a local error touches other departments (e.g., an HR delay bleeding into IT Security protocol and Data Privacy compliance).
2. Distribute the allegation horizontally across multiple parallel nodes. Contact the heads of each intersecting department simultaneously, assigning them only the specific fractional piece of the liability that falls strictly within their jurisdiction.
3. Allow each node to absorb and defend their isolated piece of the problem, ensuring they blame the originating node to clear their own localized metrics.

## Goal
To upgrade a localized management error into a documented, multi-departmental systemic failure. By spreading the pressure evenly, individual managers are more likely to admit to fractional failures to protect their own specific assets. Aggregating these micro-admissions creates undeniable leverage and proves the entire organizational architecture is compromised.

## Operational Logic
* Vertical escalation forces a single supervisor to defend their entire unit, often resulting in an immediate defensive blockade.
* Horizontal distribution lowers the individual threat assessment for each separate node. They will readily throw an adjacent department under the bus to prove their own specific silo is functioning correctly.
* Once multiple nodes from different departments formally acknowledge their fractional involvement in the failure, the organization can no longer dismiss the event as an isolated anomaly. It mathematically becomes a "systematic failure," granting you maximum leverage over the overarching corporate entity.
