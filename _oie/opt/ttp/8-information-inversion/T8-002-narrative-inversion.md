## Procedure
1. Allow the adversary's legal or compliance representative to issue a formal defensive statement attempting to minimize the incident.
2. Analyze the defensive response for partial admissions of localized errors or internal miscommunications used to dismiss claims of intentional malice.
3. Invert the defense by formally accepting their admission of error as absolute, legally binding proof of the systemic failure you initially reported.

## Goal
To weaponize the adversary's own legal defense against them. By forcing their counsel to admit to incompetence to avoid a charge of fraud, you secure documented confirmation of the compliance breach directly from their highest authority.

## Operational Logic
* Legal proxies frequently admit to administrative errors as a tactic to dismiss claims of intentional, coordinated malice.
* These admissions of error perfectly validate the core premise of a systemic compliance failure and regulatory breach.
* Extracting and reframing this admission neutralizes the lawyer's defensive posture and definitively seals the organizational liability using their own words.
