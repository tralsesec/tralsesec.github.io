# OIE-201: Unmonitored Divergence from Architectural Standard ('Process Drift')

## Definition
The entropic decay of organizational execution, characterized by a widening delta between officially documented architectural standards and the actual, undocumented workflows executed by the workforce.

## Forensic Characteristics
* **Trigger:** The introduction of inefficient official protocols, or the lack of continuous verification mechanisms following an architectural rollout.
* **Failure State:** Official documentation degrades into "legacy theory." Operators construct undocumented heuristic shortcuts or "Shadow IT" pipelines to bypass organizational bottlenecks. Management falsely calculates risk and resource allocation based on a standard operating procedure that no longer physically exists.

## Remediation
Implement continuous Organizational Pentesting to actively map the true execution footprint. Synchronize the documented protocol with the operational reality by either formalizing the efficient, operator-created shortcuts into the official architecture, or surgically refactoring the broken standard process.
